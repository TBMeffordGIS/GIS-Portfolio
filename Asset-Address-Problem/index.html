The Asset Address Problem

A GIS research project exploring how utility infrastructure can be made easier to locate, understand, and verify through human-readable asset location context.

Project Summary

Utility assets are often identified through asset IDs, coordinates, map references, and nearby street addresses. While these references are useful inside GIS and asset management systems, they do not always communicate clear field context to technicians, contractors, or operational staff.

This project explores how GIS records, metadata, 360° imagery, visual landmarks, and field context can work together to create a more human-readable approach to asset location.

Problem

Traditional location references can require field personnel to interpret coordinates, search the surrounding area, and verify the correct asset in the field. This can introduce delays, confusion, and unnecessary search time.

Concept

Instead of relying only on asset IDs, GPS coordinates, and street references, a human-readable asset location workflow adds imagery, visual landmarks, metadata, and field context.

Workflow

  • Asset ID stored in an enterprise GIS or asset management system.
  • Geographic coordinates used for mapping and routing.
  • Linked 360° imagery provides visual context.
  • Metadata describes capture date, camera orientation, and field conditions.
  • Field personnel visually confirm the correct asset before or during arrival.

Why It Matters

This approach does not replace GIS, coordinates, or asset management systems. It complements them by making spatial information easier for people to interpret in real-world operational settings.

The result is a stronger connection between enterprise GIS records, field operations, visual confirmation, and utility asset management.

Technologies and Concepts

  • GIS asset records
  • Utility infrastructure mapping
  • Metadata
  • 360° imagery
  • Field operations
  • Human-readable spatial context
